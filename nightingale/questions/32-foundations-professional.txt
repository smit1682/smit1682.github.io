Q: In which year was Florence Nightingale born?

B) 1820
C) 1830
D) 1840
Ans: B
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Florence Nightingale was born on 12 May 1820 in Florence, Italy. She is regarded as the founder of modern nursing, and her birthday is celebrated worldwide as International Nurses Day.

Q: Which famous book was written by Florence Nightingale?

B) Textbook of Medicine
C) Principles of Surgery
D) Notes on Nursing
Ans: D
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Florence Nightingale wrote "Notes on Nursing: What It Is and What It Is Not" in 1859. It outlined her ideas on patient care and the environment and is considered a foundational nursing text.

Q: Florence Nightingale is popularly remembered by which title?
A) Sister of Mercy
B) The First Matron
C) The Lady with the Lamp
D) Mother of India
Ans: C
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: She earned the title "The Lady with the Lamp" for making night rounds to care for wounded soldiers during the Crimean War. This image came to symbolize compassionate nursing care.

Q: During which war did Florence Nightingale gain fame for organizing the care of wounded soldiers?
A) Boer War
B) American Civil War
C) Crimean War
D) World War I
Ans: C
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: Nightingale served at Scutari during the Crimean War (1853-1856), where she sharply reduced mortality by improving sanitation and hygiene. Her work established the value of trained nursing and environmental reform.

Q: Communication conveyed through gestures, posture, and facial expressions is called:
A) Non-verbal communication
B) Written communication
C) Verbal communication
D) Formal communication
Ans: A
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Non-verbal communication transmits messages without words, using body language, gestures, eye contact, and facial expressions. It often conveys emotions more accurately than spoken words.

Q: Florence Nightingale's Environmental Theory primarily focuses on:
A) Manipulating the environment to promote healing
B) Genetic counselling of patients
C) Complex surgical techniques
D) Advanced pharmacological therapy
Ans: A
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: Nightingale's theory holds that a clean, well-ventilated, and calm environment supports the patient's natural reparative processes. The nurse's role is to alter the environment to promote recovery.

Q: Which of the following is NOT one of the major environmental components emphasized in Nightingale's theory?
A) Pure fresh air
B) Genetic inheritance
C) Pure water
D) Adequate light
Ans: B
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: Nightingale identified factors such as pure air, pure water, efficient drainage, cleanliness, and light as essential to health. Genetic inheritance was not part of her environmental framework.

Q: Which of the following is a characteristic of a profession?
A) Complete lack of autonomy
B) A specialized body of knowledge and a code of ethics
C) Absence of a code of ethics
D) No systematic body of knowledge
Ans: B
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: A profession is characterized by a unique body of knowledge, prolonged specialized education, a code of ethics, autonomy, and a service orientation. Nursing meets these criteria as a profession.

Q: The International Council of Nurses (ICN), the first international professional nursing organization, was founded in:

Topic: Nursing Foundations

Q: The basic elements of the communication process include:
A) Sender, message, channel, receiver, and feedback
B) Only the sender and the receiver
C) Only feedback and silence
D) Only the message and noise
Ans: A
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Effective communication involves a sender who encodes a message, a channel or medium, a receiver who decodes it, and feedback. Barriers or "noise" may interfere at any stage of this process.

Q: A noisy environment that interferes with the transmission of a message is an example of a:
A) Encoding process
B) Feedback loop
C) Communication barrier
D) Communication channel
Ans: C
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: Anything that distorts or blocks the message, such as noise, language differences, or strong emotions, is a communication barrier. Recognizing barriers helps the nurse improve clarity and understanding.

Q: Which of the following is a therapeutic communication technique?
A) Active listening
B) Giving false reassurance
C) Changing the subject
D) Giving unsolicited advice
Ans: A
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: Active listening, using silence, reflection, and open-ended questions are therapeutic techniques that encourage the patient to express feelings. False reassurance and advice-giving are non-therapeutic responses.

Q: Which of the following is a method of health teaching?

Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Lecture, group discussion, demonstration, and role play are recognized methods of health teaching. Instruments such as the stethoscope and thermometer are clinical tools, not teaching methods.

Ans: B
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: easy
Exp: Bloom's taxonomy classifies learning into cognitive (knowledge), affective (attitudes), and psychomotor (skills) domains. Nurse educators frame their teaching objectives around all three domains.

Q: Learning that involves knowledge, comprehension, and intellectual skills belongs to which domain?

Ans: B
Topic: Nursing Foundations
Diff: medium
Exp: The cognitive domain deals with mental processes such as knowledge, comprehension, application, analysis, synthesis, and evaluation. It is concerned with intellectual understanding and thinking.
